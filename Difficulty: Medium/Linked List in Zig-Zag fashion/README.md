<h2><a href="https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/problems/linked-list-in-zig-zag-fashion/1">Linked List in Zig-Zag fashion</a></h2><h3>Difficulty Level : Difficulty: Medium</h3><hr><div class="problems_problem_content__Xm_eO" style="--text-color: var(--problem-text-color);"><p><span style="font-size: 18px;">Given the <strong>head </strong>of a linked list, rearrange the nodes to form a <strong>zig-zag </strong>pattern: a ≤ b ≥ c ≤ d ≥ e ≤ f ...&nbsp; It means the first pair (a, b) is increasing, second pair (b, c)&nbsp; is decreasing, third pair (c, d) is increasing and so on in the modified linked list.</span></p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: 18px;">Only swapping of adjacent nodes is allowed.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 18px;">Y</span><span style="font-size: 18px;">ou may swap also already swapped adjacent but cannot swap two nodes which are not adjacent.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;">For example, for the linked list: </span><span style="font-size: 18px;">11 -&gt; 15 -&gt; 20 -&gt; 5 -&gt; 10</span></p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: 18px;">11 -&gt; 20 -&gt; 5 -&gt; 15 -&gt; 10 is valid, while</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 18px;">5 -&gt; 20 -&gt; 11 -&gt; 15 -&gt; 10 is invalid because it changes the relative order of nodes.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;">Return the head of the modified zig-zag linked list.</span></p>
